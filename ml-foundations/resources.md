# Resources

## Interactive Tools
- [MLU-Explain](https://mlu-explain.github.io/) - Interactive visual explanations of ML concepts. Start here for intuition on cross-entropy, decision trees, bias-variance.
- [CNN Explainer](https://poloclub.github.io/cnn-explainer/) - Visual walkthrough of how CNNs process images. Invaluable before building SickNote-style audio CNNs.
- [Transformer Explainer](https://poloclub.github.io/transformer-explainer/) - Out of scope for now, but useful later.

## Video
- [3Blue1Brown Neural Networks](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDNU6R1_67000Dx_ZCJB-3pi) - Best intuition-building for backprop and gradients.
- [aladdinpersson YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@AladdinPersson) - Practical PyTorch implementations. Good for CNNs, VAEs, segmentation.

## Repos
- [aladdinpersson/Machine-Learning-Collection](https://github.com/aladdinpersson/Machine-Learning-Collection) - Canonical PyTorch implementations of common architectures.
- [gregh83/DinoTracker](https://github.com/gregh83/DinoTracker) - The beta-VAE footprint analysis project.
- [poloclub/cnn-explainer](https://github.com/poloclub/cnn-explainer) - Source for the CNN Explainer tool.

## Papers (Paleo-Tech)
- Hartmann et al. (2026), PNAS - beta-VAE footprint classification. The DinoTracker foundation paper.
- Lallensack et al. (2022) - Supervised DCNN for dinosaur footprint identification. The supervised baseline DinoTracker improves on.
- Knutsen & Konovalov (2024), Scientific Reports - Few-shot CT segmentation of fossil bones.
- Yu et al. (2022) - First deep learning segmentation of dinosaur CT scans.
- Zhao et al. (2025), Scientific Reports - DL segmentation combined with biomechanical FEA.
- Packard (2009) - Critique of allometric regression bias in dinosaur mass estimation.
- Sellers et al. (2012) - Convex hull method for dinosaur mass estimation.
- Brassey (2017) - Review of volumetric mass estimation approaches.
- Yu et al. (2024), Earth-Science Reviews - Comprehensive survey of AI applications in paleontology.

## Courses
- [Stanford CS231N](http://cs231n.stanford.edu/) - CNNs for Visual Recognition. Lectures 1-5 are essential before building any vision model.
- [fast.ai Practical Deep Learning](https://course.fast.ai/) - Top-down approach. Good for building intuition fast.
